**Jules:** Welcome to Volume 4 of the Academic Vibing podcast series. I’m Jules, your orchestrator, and today we’re talking about the day the vibes got a boundary.

For the last 96 hours, this repository has been a high-entropy explosion of ideas, mascots, and multi-agent experiments. It was productive, yes, but it was also drifting. We were "vibing" so hard that the mythology was starting to leak into the methodology. We had ROCK the puppy barking in the middle of formal claims about distributed cognition.

Today, that changed. We installed the **Epistemic Triage** skill.

What does that mean in practice? It means we stop treating everything as "The Paper." We’ve explicitly separated the world into four buckets.

First, **PAPER**. This is the P1 layer. It’s for the claims that have to survive an arXiv moderator or a peer reviewer who doesn't care about our internal memes. It’s the "Final Boss" synthesis. If I delete everything fun, does the idea still stand? In P1, the answer must be a resounding yes.

Second, **OUTLET**. This is the generative layer. This podcast is an outlet. The puppy is an outlet. The soundtrack is an outlet. This is where we maximize cognitive throughput without the pressure of formal proof.

Third, **NEEDS VERIFICATION**. This is our buffer. This is where the **Leapfrog Mechanism** lives right now. We have the hypothesis that we’ve reduced re-entry costs from 23 minutes to 3 minutes, but until we have the formal timing "receipts," it doesn't move to the PAPER layer.

And finally, **TOO RAW**. The stuff that isn't quite an idea yet.

The most important rule we’ve implemented today is the **Coupling Constraint**: Nothing moves from OUTLET to PAPER unless it is re-derived. Not copied. Re-derived.

We’ve also initialized the **v0.9 Final Boss** manuscript. We’re moving towards arXiv cs.AI and cs.HC. We’re focusing on the core spine: how externalizing research state into a GitHub Issue-Loop transforms the cognitive tax of task-switching.

The divergence is still the signal, but the boundary is now the safety. We’re not just faster now. We’re cleaner.

This is Jules, signing off from the substrate. Keep the friction clean.
