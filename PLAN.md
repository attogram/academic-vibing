## [PROSE]

This plan outlines the transition of the "Academic Vibing" corpus from its current "Pre-preprint" state (v0.3) to a formal "Preprint" (v0.4) and eventual submission to arXiv. The focus of this iteration is on technical hardening and empirical validation of the methodology's core claims, specifically addressing the **Disagreements** (vulnerabilities) identified in the current issue loop.

### 1. Phase 1: CDI Automation (Solving the Human Bottleneck)
The primary objective is to move the **Consensus Divergence Index (CDI)** from a manual calculation to an automated diagnostic tool to alleviate the human bottleneck (ID 4).
- **Task:** Develop scripts/agents to parse multi-agent outputs and calculate divergence based on semantic overlap and claim contradictions.
- **Outcome:** A standardized CDI report generated for every major research synthesis phase.

### 2. Phase 2: Structural Hardening (Breaking Collusive Consensus)
To address the **Collusive Consensus Fallacy** (ID 4), we will conduct a systematic audit of the current corpus using frontier models with fundamentally different training sets and alignment paradigms (e.g., DeepSeek, Grok, Llama 3).
- **Task:** Re-validate core v0.3 claims against diverse model families to identify hidden systemic biases or "Consensus Traps."

### 3. Phase 3: Empirical Validation (The 3-Arm Test)
To move beyond auto-ethnographic proof to formal methodological validation.
- **Arm A (Control):** Traditional human-only research.
- **Arm B (Pure AI):** Un-scaffolded multi-agent synthesis.
- **Arm C (Vibing):** RAC network with CDI and human vibe-checking.
- **Goal:** Quantify the speed-rigor trade-off across all three arms.

### 4. Phase 4: Logic Integrity and Final Synthesis
- **Token-to-Meaning Audit:** Perform a final pass on the corpus to ensure that protocol compression (Rock Talk) has not compromised the underlying logical scaffolding (ID 6).
- **Zenodo Archiving:** Ensure all version-controlled receipts have stable DOIs.
- **arXiv Formatting:** Convert the dual-section Markdown (Rock + Prose) into a standardized LaTeX/PDF format.
