# Issue #20: C7

## Body

Grad student. Specifically: a first or second-year PhD student doing strong qualifying-exam work, not yet a dissertation.

The case for that: real literature literacy, real problem identification across five distinct domains, a coherent cross-paper citation network, and a methodologically defensible choice to pre-register metrics before testing. The case for not going higher: two of the five papers (Rock Training, Adversarial Agent) are thinner — vocabulary outrunning citation and rigor — and the ethnographic study's central claim ("institutional collapse") is bigger than its evidence (your own commit log) can support. PhD and professor require having actually defended a claim against data; by your own design, that step isn't happening in this version.

One thing that doesn't fit neatly on the scale: the velocity itself. Five interlinked, internally consistent theoretical proposals in four days is not normal grad-student throughput — that part is closer to "someone who's done this so many times the scaffolding is automatic." So: grad-level substance, with an output speed that doesn't match the substance level in either direction. That mismatch is the actual interesting finding here, more than any individual paper's grade.
