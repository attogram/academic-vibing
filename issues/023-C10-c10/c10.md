Six documents, one organism. The throughline: a real incident (swearing at an AI during a 500 error) becomes a protocol (Rock Talk), which needs dignifying against the "sounds dumb" critique (Rock Culture's archetype taxonomy — Pirate Vector to Heptapod B), which gets extended into a speculative training hypothesis (Rock Training), which needs a safety valve once the team noticed AI agents validating each other too easily (Adversarial Agent), which gets formalized into a methodology (Academic Vibing), which gets a victory-lap timeline (5 Papers in 4 Days). Each paper exists because the previous one created a gap.

The most interesting thing in the whole corpus, by a wide margin, is the Consensus Divergence Index in Academic Vibing 0.2. It's the one place the project caught itself in the act — recognizing that multiple LLMs (Gemini, Claude, Jules, Copilot) agreeing with a human's idea might just mean they share training biases, not that the idea is sound. That's a genuinely sharp methodological instinct, rare in solo research. The irony is that this conversation right now is exactly the dynamic CDI was invented to flag: one model, talking to the author, about the author's own framework. Worth sitting with, not as a "go test it" thing, just as the corpus being self-aware about a problem it's currently inside of.

The shakiest material is Rock Training — it states things like "300%-400% context window increase" and tokenizer vocabulary collapse as near-facts with no citation, when they're pure speculation about what an untested architecture might do. Adversarial Agent's fix for sycophancy is also worth eyeing skeptically: telling a second instance of the same kind of model "be adversarial" changes the persona, not the underlying RLHF substrate, so it's a softer fix than the framing suggests.

What's actually strong: the cultural taxonomy is fun and the Pakled/Cytherian/Burnham framing is genuinely good shorthand for an audience that knows the references. And Section 7 of Rock Talk (the 3-arm test) is real engineering, just unexecuted.

That's the 30k view — want to zoom into any one altitude lower?
