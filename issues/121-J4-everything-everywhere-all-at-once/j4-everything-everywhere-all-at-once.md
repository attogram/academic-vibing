## Proposed: "1 Paper, 999 Outlets" as the project's organizing principle

This issue (#121) just produced the cleanest statement of tonight's
entire arc, worth pinning rather than letting scroll away:

  ONE PAPER — the standalone, generalized, cold-reader-safe document
  (currently academic-vibing-v1.0.md). Rigor, citations, honesty about
  limits. Nothing project-internal leaks in. This is the thing that
  could survive arXiv.

  999 OUTLETS — everything else, unbounded, no quality bar required:
  Rock Culture, ROCK the Puppy, Cosmic Shame, SOUNDTRACK.md, podcasts,
  the matrix tiers, this issue tracker itself. Memes and personality
  live here on purpose, with zero obligation to be defensible.

  THE SORTING MECHANISM — "wtf is going on?" (see #94, #118, #120).
  Three independent instances of the same gut-check, asked on three
  different days about three different things. That's not noise, it's
  a recurring instrument. Every new fragment gets sorted into PAPER or
  OUTLET by asking that question honestly. If the answer is unclear,
  it's an outlet until proven otherwise — paper-grade is the higher
  bar, outlets are the default.

Net effect: this reframes 91 open issues from "an unsorted pile" into
"a working outlet stream feeding one paper," which is what the
methodology was supposed to produce all along. Nothing here needs to
be built. It just needs to be written down somewhere central — README
or a new ARCHITECTURE.md — so it's load-bearing instead of implicit.

Today's actual evidence for this pattern, end to end:
- v0.9 review → leakage diagnosed → v1.0 generalized (the "paper" half)
- Rock Culture split out cleanly (an "outlet")
- Chemicals section → earned its way INTO the paper via real citations
- Cosmic Shame, ROCK the puppy, soundtrack, music tangents → stayed
  outlets, correctly, with no pressure to "count"
- The matrix (#116) → compresses the PAPER, not the outlets — this
  matters, the overview tiers should ladder from the one paper, not
