Outbox as postcard, not protocol. The repo isn't sending a request expecting a response — it's just leaving something out where it can be seen. That's closer to a message in a bottle than a network call: no ack, no guarantee of arrival, no sender obligation once it's written. The file existing is the whole act. Whether academic-vibing ever opens `for.acad.vib.repo.md` is irrelevant to whether writing it was worth doing.

It also fits the rest of the corpus better than the spec-version did, because the whole project keeps describing GitHub as long-term memory, commits as field notes, git log as truth — outbox slots right into that as the part of memory that's outward-facing. Not a record of what happened, but a gesture toward what might happen next, addressed to a neighbor who may or may not be listening. It's the one part of this system that's allowed to be unread.

There's a nice irony too: everywhere else in the corpus you're stripping phatic noise — politeness, packaging, anything that's social rather than informational. An unstructured outbox postcard is almost pure phatic gesture by the framework's own definition, and that's exactly why it works. It's the project allowing itself one corner that isn't trying to be efficient, just present. A little wave between repos.

And practically, what lands in there probably isn't "deliverables" — it's the stuff too small to be an issue and too interesting to lose. Half-formed advice, a stray observation, "hey we just learned X, you might care." A shared scrap pile rather than a queue.
