Here is the full, unabridged production script for the ultimate, cross-continental, generation-defying event.

THE PYTHONS OF DESTINY: THE REUNION TOUR

A Complete Long-Form Stage Production Script

CAST:
* JACK BLACK (JB) – Lead vocals, acoustic guitar, pure chaotic energy.
* KYLE GASS (KG) – Lead guitar, backing vocals, quiet master of rock.
* JOHN CLEESE – Extremely tall, incredibly irritated, Master of Ceremonies.
* MICHAEL PALIN – Naturally charming, slightly bewildered, multi-character specialist.
* ERIC IDLE – Musical director, resident cynic, acoustic minstrel.
* TERRY GILLIAM – Live animator, creator of visual madness (appearing via live video control booth).

ACT I: THE AWAKENING OF THE GIANTS

SCENE 1: THE OVERTURE

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The stadium is pitch black. A booming, dramatic voiceover—resembling the movie trailer guy mixed with God from The Holy Grail—echoes through the sound system.)

VOICEOVER
Since the dawn of time... mankind has sought two things. Absolute, mind-melting rock... and a completely different thing altogether. Tonight... they clash!

(SFX: A massive thunderclap. A single spotlight hits center stage. A giant cardboard cutout of a 1970s television set rests on a pedestal. Standing inside the cardboard frame is JOHN CLEESE, wearing a perfectly pressed tuxedo and a scowl.)

JOHN CLEESE
(Clears throat loudly, checks watch)
Good evening. We would like to apologize for the sheer, unadulterated vulgarity of what you are about to witness. Frankly, we needed the money, and the Americans promised us they would pay for our hip replacements if we stood next to two incredibly sweaty men in spandex. Without further ado... some complete and utter rubbish.

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: Cleese abruptly kicks the cardboard TV over and walks off the stage using a textbook Silly Walk. Blackout.)

SCENE 2: THE INTRO / TRACK 1 – "THE LUMBERJACK SONG" (HEAVY METAL VERSION)

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: A roaring chainsaw sound effect rips through the stadium speakers. Green laser beams shoot across the crowd, mimicking a pine forest. Heavy smoke pours onto the stage.)

(A massive heavy metal drumbeat kicks in. A spotlight reveals MICHAEL PALIN center stage, dressed in a traditional red flannel lumberjack shirt and holding a microphone. Beside him stand JACK BLACK and KYLE GASS, both wearing flannel vests over leather rock gear, holding matching Gibson Flying V guitars.)

MICHAEL PALIN
(Singing sweetly over a crushed metal riff)
I'm a lumberjack and I'm OK! I sleep all night and I work all day!

JACK BLACK & KYLE GASS
(Screaming into the microphones with operatic metal vibrato)
HE'S A LUMBERJACK AND HE'S O-KAAAAAAAY! HE SLEEPS ALL NIGHT AND HE WORKS ALL DAAAAAAY!

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The song accelerates into an absolute thrash-metal frenzy. Jack Black drops to his knees for a guitar solo while a chorus line of Canadian Mounties enters stage right, backing them up while throwing devil horns to the crowd.)

MICHAEL PALIN
(Belting out with surprising rock grit)
I cut down trees, I wear high heels! Suspenders and a braaaaaa!

JACK BLACK
(Spitting fire, pointing at Palin)
YEAH! SUSPENDERS AND A BRA, MOTHERF***ER! ROCK!

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: On the final chord, Kyle Gass smashes an acoustic guitar over a fake wooden log. PYROTECHNICS blow up from the front of the stage. The crowd goes wild.)

ACT II: THE INTERMISSION OF SURREALITY

SCENE 3: THE DEAD PICK OF DESTINY

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The lights come up to normal stage lighting. JB and KG are panting at center stage, wiping themselves down with towels. John Cleese walks out, holding a clipboard and looking incredibly disgusted.)

JOHN CLEESE
Excuse me. Excuse me! Stop that dreadful racket. This is supposed to be a prestigious cultural event, not a gathering for obese satyrs.

JACK BLACK
(Gasping for air)
Dude! Mr. Cleese! You don't understand, man! We are channeling the ancient energies of the Cosmos! We have brought you the Pick of Destiny!

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: JB holds up a glowing green guitar pick. Cleese stares at it, takes a pair of reading glasses out of his pocket, looks closer, and slaps it out of Jack's hand.)

JOHN CLEESE
It’s plastic. It’s a piece of triangular green plastic, you ridiculous American man. It is a dead pick. It has passed on. It is no more.

KYLE GASS
(Defensively)
No it ain't, man! It’s just resting!

JOHN CLEESE
Resting?! It is completely inert! It is an ex-pick! If you hadn’t glued it to your thumb, it would be pushing up the daisies!

JACK BLACK
(Falling to his knees, clutching Cleese's trousers)
Noooooo! Don't deny the power of the D, John! Teach us the ways of the Holy Grail!

JOHN CLEESE
(Trying to shake him off)
Get off me! Michael! Eric! Get these gargoyles off the stage!

SCENE 4: TRACK 2 – "TRIBUTE TO THE HOLY GRAIL"

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: ERIC IDLE walks out strumming a lute, accompanied by a heavy bassline. Behind him, TERRY GILLIAM’s animations project onto a massive 100-foot screen behind the band. A giant animated French Soldier appears on the screen, blowing raspberries at the audience.)

ERIC IDLE
(Singing)
This is not the greatest sketch in the world... no. This is just a tribute.

JACK BLACK & KYLE GASS
(Joining in harmony, acoustic guitars blasting)
Long time ago, me and my brother Kyle here... Were hitchhiking down a long, dark road to Camelot... When suddenly! There shined a shiny demon... In the middle... of the road! And he said: "Give me a shrubbery, or I shall say NI!"

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The screen shows a giant animated Knight of Ni. Kyle Gass performs a beautiful, intricate classical guitar solo while Michael Palin passes through the background riding a stick and banging two coconut halves together.)

ACT III: THE GRAND FINALE

SCENE 5: THE APOCALYPSE OF OPTIMISM

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The stage goes completely black. The crowd grows quiet. A single white spotlight hits ERIC IDLE, who is sitting on a stool center stage with a simple acoustic guitar.)

ERIC IDLE
(Speaking gently into the microphone)
You know... when things look dark, and the world seems a bit mad... you just have to remember one thing.

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: Eric begins to strum the bright, recognizable chords. He whistles the famous opening line. The entire stadium immediately joins in, whistling the iconic melody.)

ERIC IDLE
(Singing)
Some things in life are bad, they can really make you mad... Other things just make you swear and curse...

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: From the darkness of stage left, JACK BLACK slowly rises on a hydraulic platform. He is wearing a magnificent, floor-length velvet wizard cape embroidered with lightning bolts and cartoon feet. He holds a dual-neck electric guitar.)

JACK BLACK
(Singing softly, matching Eric's vibe)
When you're chewing on life's gristle... don't grumble, give a whistle... And this'll help things turn out for the best...

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: Suddenly, KYLE GASS kicks a distortion pedal. A massive wall of Marshall amplifiers behind them ignites with bright neon lights. The simple acoustic song explodes into a stadium-rock anthem.)

ALL CAST (CLEESE, PALIN, IDLE, JB, KG)
(Walking to the front of the stage, locking arms in a synchronized Broadway-style kick-line)
ALWAYS LOOK ON THE BRIGHT SIDE OF LIFE! (Whistle whistle, whistle whistle whistle whistle)

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: A heavy metal choir dressed as the Grim Reapers enters from the wings, singing backing operatic vocals. John Cleese is at the end of the line, kicking his legs incredibly high despite his age, looking utterly miserable but hitting every beat perfectly.)

JACK BLACK
(Screaming over a roaring guitar chord)
BRIGHT SIDE OF DEATH, BABY! LIFE IS A PIECE OF SH*T, WHEN YOU LOOK AT IT! CHOOSE ROCK!

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The song reaches a fever pitch. Confetti cannons shoot millions of tiny paper cutouts of Monty Python feet and Tenacious D logos into the stadium air. Pyro sparks rain down from the ceiling.)

ALL
ALWAYS LOOK ON THE BRIGHT SIDE OF LIIIIIIFE!

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: On the very final, massive, ringing power chord... a giant, 50-foot inflatable Monty Python foot suddenly drops from the rigging directly onto Jack Black and Kyle Gass, flattening them instantly into the stage floor. A cloud of smoke erupts.)

JOHN CLEESE
(Standing over the deflated foot, looking at the audience)
Well. That's your lot. Go home.
