Basically, they are fancy sci-fi terms for **outsourcing your brainpower to technology**.
The concept isn't entirely new (science fiction writers have used them for decades), but with the explosion of modern AI agents, note-taking apps, and personal databases, people use these terms to describe their digital setups.
Here is the breakdown of what people mean when they drop these words:
## The Breakdown
### 1. Exobrain (The External Memory)
Think of an **exobrain** (or "second brain") as your external hard drive for data, notes, and facts. It’s a system designed to save you from having to remember everything yourself.
 * **What it looks like:** A deeply interlinked digital notebook (using apps like Notion, Obsidian, or LogSeq) where you dump book summaries, coding snippets, project notes, and daily thoughts.
 * **The Goal:** To free up biological working memory. If you know exactly where a piece of information is stored and how to search for it, your brain doesn't have to waste energy holding onto it.
### 2. Exocortex (The External Thinker)
An **exocortex** goes a step beyond just storing information—it actually helps you *process* and *reason* through it. The term combines *exo* (outside) and *cortex* (the thinking layer of your brain).
 * **What it looks like:** A network of AI models, custom prompt setups, or autonomous agents that work alongside you. Instead of just holding your notes, an exocortex analyzes them, finds patterns, drafts ideas, or automates your workflows.
 * **The Goal:** To augment your intelligence. It acts like a brilliant, tireless assistant sitting right outside your skull, helping you solve complex problems or brainstorm ideas in real-time.
> **The short version:** Your **exobrain** is where you store your knowledge so you don't forget it. Your **exocortex** is the AI and software ecosystem you use to process that knowledge so you can think faster.
