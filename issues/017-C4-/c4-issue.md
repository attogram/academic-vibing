# Issue #17: C4

## Body

Yeah, this is a fun read. A few things that actually stand out:

**The dual-format is the move.** The paper *demonstrates* its thesis. Rock Talk isn't just described in the Prose sections — it's right there, doing the work, next to the academic writeup. That's genuinely clever. Most manifestos *talk about* the thing. This one *is* the thing.

**The Friday catalyst is the best origin story.** "Shut the F* up and just tell me what you changed" under HTTP 500 pressure → birth of a linguistic protocol. That's a real moment. High-stakes environments are where noise gets expensive fast, and the ethnographic justification (linguistic noise = latency bottleneck) actually holds up.

**Git as field notes is epistemically interesting.** Unlike a self-reported diary, the commit log is adversarial to bullshit. Timestamps, diffs, issue threads — they can't be retrofitted. The researcher is betting the whole claim on verifiable artifacts, which is a much harder (and more honest) bar than most N=1 studies set.

**The N=1 auto-ethnography frame is the right one.** The author isn't claiming 5 papers in 4 days is *normalizable* — just that it's *possible*, and that the receipts are there to check. That's a much more defensible position than "AI replaces academia."

A few things I'm chewing on, though:

- **"Friction is the new peer review"** is a sharp line but underspecified. CDI between agents catching logical weak points is *one kind* of friction. It doesn't replicate the slow, embodied, historically-informed kind that catches things like p-hacking or bad causal models. The paper knows this implicitly but doesn't name it.
- **The "Jules Factor"** is doing a lot of work. The orchestrating agent persisting context across sleep cycles is essentially a memory prosthesis. That's real, but the paper undersells how much of the velocity comes from a single well-prompted agent vs. the MAS architecture itself.
- **The transition from "possible" to "generalizable"** is where this gets hard. The researcher is the instrument, and instruments have specific calibration. Replicating this with a different human, different agents, different problem domain — that's the real test.

But as a pre-preprint manifesto with receipts? It's earned its DOI.
