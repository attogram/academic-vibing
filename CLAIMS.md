# Claims: Academic Vibing 0.1

This document lists the primary claims made in the Academic Vibing 0.1 meta-methodology.

## Methodological Claims

1.  **Meta-Methodology for High-Speed Research:** Academic Vibing is a valid and effective meta-methodology for high-speed agentic research and structured curiosity.
2.  **Emergent Rigor:** Rigor in AI-assisted research can emerge through recursive agent consensus and high-frequency feedback loops rather than traditional institutional processes.
3.  **Structured Curiosity:** Systematic exploration of a hypothesis through high-bandwidth interaction with multiple autonomous agents is a disciplined and productive process.
4.  **Recursive Agent Consensus (RAC):** The overlap of outputs from multiple high-performing models (e.g., Jules, Gemini, Claude) serves as a functional proxy for peer review in technical domains.
5.  **Friction as Signal:** Areas of disagreement between disparate LLM models are high-signal "friction points" that reliably identify areas requiring human investigation.
6.  **Externalized Long-Term Memory:** The "GitHub Issue-Loop" effectively solves context-window limitations and maintains MAS coordination across sessions.
7.  **Voice-to-Rock Accelerator:** Voice-to-text dictation acts as a cognitive filter that naturally enforces the compressed Rock Talk protocol.

## Operational Claims

8.  **Low-Latency Effectiveness:** The methodology is specifically effective in high-stakes, low-latency technical environments where traditional research is too slow.
9.  **Zero-Budget Viability:** High-signal research can be conducted using a low-cost, mobile-first hardware stack (e.g., phone + laptop + free-tier APIs).
10. **Hardware Independence:** The efficiency of the protocol and methodology is more critical than raw compute power for generating research outcomes.
