# Academic Vibing 0.8: Structured Curiosity for Human-Agent Collaboration (4k context)

Academic Vibing 0.8 represents the maturation of a rapid-iteration research methodology designed for LLMs and autonomous agents.

Methodology: RAC (Recursive Agent Consensus) among disparate models (Jules, Gemini, Claude). CDI (Consensus Divergence Index) measures friction. High CDI indicates signal; Low CDI flags a "Consensus Trap" or shared training bias.

Persistence: GitHub Issue-Loop serves as external long-term memory. Standardized schema ([STATE], [BLOCKER], [PAYLOAD]) prevent prose leakage and enable headless MAS coordination. Solving "Token-to-Meaning Fallacy" via Internal CoT + External Rock Talk compression.

Leapfrog Mechanism (F2): The ability to drop and re-enter context at zero cost. UCI research (Mark et al., 2008) cites 23-minute recovery; Academic Vibing reduces this to ~3 minutes via exocognitive state store. Productivity-wellbeing surplus is causally downstream of re-entry freedom.

Adversarial Rigor: Institutional Trauma (Wikipedia/HN). Verifiability (Wiki) and Subjective Interestingness (HN) are vulnerable to MAS simulation. Verification flood and "Bad Faith Swarms" (E10) poison consensus. Defense: Friction-First auditing (CDI). Simple agreement is no longer a safety signal.

Mascot: ROCK the Puppy (symbolizing untrained agentic enthusiasm).
