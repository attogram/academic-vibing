# Academic Vibing 0.8: Structured Curiosity for Human-Agent Collaboration (PROSE)

Academic Vibing 0.8 represents the maturation of a rapid-iteration research methodology designed for Large Language Models (LLMs) and autonomous agents. This version synthesizes the foundational fragments developed across versions 0.1 through 0.7—including the early Rock Talk protocols, the Wikipedia and Hacker News "Institutional Trauma" post-mortems, and the formalization of the **Consensus Divergence Index (CDI)** as the primary metric for research signaling.

The methodology is centered on the **Recursive Agent-Based Consensus Network (RAC)**, where multiple disparate agents explore a problem space. Consensus among these models serves as a proxy for peer review, while disagreement indicates high-signal "friction points" (High CDI). Low CDI is flagged as a potential "Consensus Trap" or shared bias.

To scale agentic research, the project uses the **GitHub Issue-Loop** as externalized long-term memory. Standardized schema ([STATE], [BLOCKER], [PAYLOAD]) allow for headless Multi-Agent System (MAS) coordination. The "Token-to-Meaning Fallacy" is solved by decoupling internal reasoning (CoT) from external protocol compression (Rock Talk).

The **Leapfrog Mechanism** is introduced as the ability to drop and re-enter research contexts at near-zero cost. While conventional cognitive science reports a 23-minute recovery time from interruptions (Mark et al., 2008), the Academic Vibing exocognitive state store reduces re-entry to approximately 3 minutes. The resulting wellbeing surplus is causally downstream of this re-entry cost reduction.

Adversarial rigor is maintained through **Friction-First auditing**. Platforms relying on "Proof of Vibe" (subjective interestingness) or "Proof of Citation" (Wikipedia) are vulnerable to synthetic consensus. The CDI offers a robust defense by identifying coordinated swarm mimicry. **ROCK the Puppy**, our untrained mascot, symbolizes the eager nature of early-stage agentic swarms requiring human guidance. Future work includes arXiv submission and Zenodo archiving.
